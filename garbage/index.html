Yala National Park

(25 reviews)

Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka, bordering the Indian Ocean. Yala was designated a wildlife sanctuary in 1900 and was designated a national park in 1938. The park is best known for its variety of wild animals. It is important for the conservation of Sri Lankan elephants, Sri Lankan leopards and aquatic birds. The park is worth visiting at all times of the year, however visiting Yala during December to April is recommended.

Temple of Tooth

(22 reviews)

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic is commonly known as the Sri Dalada Maligawa. It was built in the year 1595 during the region of King Vimaladarmasuriya. It is located in the royal palace complex of the former Kingdom of Kandy, which houses the relic of the tooth of the Buddha. The temple of the tooth is a World Heritage Site mainly due to the temple and the relic. It is a place of great historic, cultural and spiritual significance.
